Kibbeling

This is an interactive visualization of the Food.com dataset.

In the center of the page you can see a chord diagram of a couple of popular ingredients. This chord diagram shows the co-occurence of ingredients in all the recipes. If we, for instance, look at the chord between flour and salt, we can see that around 30% of the flour recipes in this graph also use salt. The other way around we can see that with these ingredients only around 10% of the salt recipes use flour.

Now try selecting one of the chords.

If you've selected a chord some new diagrams on the right should appear. These diagrams show the different cuisines, meal times and cooking times. Try clicking on some of the diagrams and see what changes.

Clicking on the diagrams applies a filter, for instance selecting a specific cuisine makes the other diagrams only look at the recipes from that cuisine.

Finally press the button near the bottom. This button gives you a list of recipes decided by the filters you applied.

Have fun using this interactive recipe finder.
